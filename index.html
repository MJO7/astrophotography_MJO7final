Primary Gear

The specific gear that is necessary for Astrophotography is as follows:

1.) Camera




In Astrophotography , most cellular phones just won't work , especially in deep sky.

What we need(and use) are DSLR cameras , with sensor sizes of 1.5x(crop) to 1x(full

frame). We also need manual control.



Settings like ISO , shutterspeed and Aperture are quite essential , so I recommend

that you learn them. We need manual control , hence a DSLR / MIRRORLESS camera

is a great choice.



My DSLR camera.I recommend starting out with an APSC DSLR rather than a full frame

DSLR since its more affordable


The Goal is to get as much as light possible to create a colorful noiseless image.

Such Settings can only be controlled via a DSLR/MIRRORLESS manual camera.

2.) Lens/Telescope



This is your main imaging equipment. A lens(or a telescope , for that

matter) focuses the light collected at night at your camera sensor.



A camera lens is an excellent starting point , and a refractor scope

an excellent choice for advanced work.Lenses are very capable and an

excellent way to use some gear one already has , being a photographer





The image you see in the background is just a representation of the

beauty of space. Do not expect such results with amateur equipment,that

is shown here.



A telescope here is useless without tracking though. Scroll down to see

the section of equatorial mounts , The one thing in your setup that will

make a difference.

3.) Equatorial Mount

Something that is absolutely necessary in astrophotography-

An equatorial mount is a device that counteracte the apparent rotation

of the globe,without which an image above 25 seconds untracked is blurry

and traily.



I do not recommend a heavy duty German equatorial mount as your device.



A star tracker is quite enough,and I have had very good success with that.
